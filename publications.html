Dr. Yethindra's research has been published in some of the most respected medical journals globally. His work spans a wide range of medical topics, including cardiology, neurology, infectious diseases, and public health. Here are some highlights

Decreased Mortality Among Hospitalized COVID-19 Patients Who Underwent Anticoagulant Therapy

Indian Journal of Pharmacology, 2020. This pivotal research explored the benefits of anticoagulants in reducing mortality rates among severe COVID-19 patients.

Exploring the Novel Yaravirus Genome

Biomedicine, 2020. Dr. Yethindra contributed to the understanding of rare viruses through his study on Yaravirus, a newly discovered microorganism, and its implications for human health.

The Role of Remdesivir in Inhibiting SARS-CoV-2

International Journal of Research in Pharmaceutical Sciences, 2020. His theoretical work on the antiviral effects of remdesivir helped in shaping early treatment protocols during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Efficacy of Umifenovir for Mild and Moderate COVID-19

International Journal of Research in Pharmaceutical Sciences, 2020. This research evaluated umifenovir’s effectiveness in treating COVID-19, suggesting it may be beneficial in reducing symptoms in mild-to-moderate cases.

Alpine Climate Therapy for Immune Thrombocytopenic Purpura in Children

International Journal of Research in Pharmaceutical Sciences, 2020. This study examined how Alpine climate therapy may aid in managing immune thrombocytopenic purpura in pediatric patients.

Potential Role of Remdesivir in COVID-19 Treatment: VYTR Hypothesis

International Journal of Research in Pharmaceutical Sciences, 2020. A theoretical analysis on remdesivir's potential to inhibit SARS-CoV and MERS-CoV, anticipating its utility in treating COVID-19.

Exploring the Novel Yaravirus Genome

Biomedicine, 2020. This study advanced the understanding of the Yaravirus genome, offering insights into virus evolution and potential implications for human health.

Neurofibromatosis Type 1 with Chromosome 17 Deletion: A Rare Case Study

Clinical Case Reports, 2021. A case report highlighting the genotype-phenotype link in neurofibromatosis, revealing a rare deletion on chromosome 17.

Mirror Writing in Frontal Lobe Epilepsy

Clinical Case Reports, 2021. This unique case study documented a patient with frontal lobe epilepsy who exhibited mirror writing, contributing to the literature on neurological manifestations.

B-Complex Vitamins and Olfactory Training for COVID-19 Anosmia

Clinical Case Reports, 2021. This report suggested B-complex vitamins and olfactory exercises as promising approaches for restoring smell in COVID-19 patients with anosmia.

Chemical Burns of the Esophagus in Kyrgyz Children: A 10-Year Analysis

Biomedicine, 2021. A comprehensive study analyzing the prevalence and causes of esophageal burns in children over a decade in Kyrgyzstan.

Effects of Itolizumab on IL-6 Levels in Severe COVID-19 Patients

Indian Journal of Pharmacology, 2021. This study explored itolizumab’s potential in reducing inflammatory IL-6 levels in critically ill COVID-19 patients.

Cardiovascular Disease and Diabetes as COVID-19 Mortality Factors: Systematic Review

American Heart Journal, 2021. A meta-analysis investigating cardiovascular disease and diabetes as predictors of COVID-19 severity and mortality.

Rapid Diagnosis of Breast Tuberculosis via Fine-Needle Aspiration

Clinical Case Reports, 2021. This study highlighted the effectiveness of fine-needle aspiration for quick and accurate diagnosis of breast tuberculosis.

Pathophysiology of Thromboembolism in COVID-19 Patients

European Journal of Clinical and Experimental Medicine, 2022. A review detailing the mechanisms behind thromboembolic complications in COVID-19 patients.

Metabolic Syndrome in Obese Adolescents: Insulin Secretion and Resistance

Metabolism, 2022. This study evaluated insulin resistance and metabolic risk factors in adolescents with obesity.

Neurological Symptoms in Pediatric COVID-19 Patients: A Case Series

Biomedicine, 2023. This case series explored neurological manifestations in children with COVID-19, contributing to a growing body of pediatric data.

Adverse Effects of Cancer Treatment in Cervical Cancer Patients

Cureus, 2024. This study examined the side effects of various cancer treatments in patients with cervical cancer, emphasizing the need for supportive care.

In Silico Vaccine Design Against Naegleria fowleri

Indian Journal of Pharmacology, 2024. Using computational methods, this study aimed to design a vaccine against Naegleria fowleri, a parasite causing fatal brain infections.

Topical Progesterone for Retinitis Pigmentosa Treatment

Indian Journal of Pharmacology, 2023. This research assessed the efficacy of progesterone as a potential therapy for retinitis pigmentosa, a degenerative eye disease.

Complete Gonadal Dysgenesis in a Patient with 46XY Karyotype

Clinical Case Reports, 2024. A rare case detailing a female patient with complete gonadal dysgenesis and a 46XY chromosomal makeup.

Cervical Cancer Complicated by Severe Bleeding: A Rare Case

Indian Journal of Gynecologic Oncology, 2024. This case report focused on a rare instance of cervical cancer with life-threatening bleeding, underscoring the need for specialized management.

Diet and Insulin Resistance in Adults at Risk for Type 2 Diabetes

Metabolism, 2024. This study evaluated dietary impacts on insulin resistance, aiming to guide preventive measures for diabetes in at-risk individuals.

Essentials of Hematology: Inside Hematology

In 2018, I wrote “Essentials of Hematology: Inside Hematology,” published in English, Hindi, and Russian. It was acknowledged as a world record for the “Youngest person to write a trilingual book.”

The Historical Development of the Heart i.e., from its Formation from…,

In 2019, I wrote “The Historical Development of the Heart i.e., from its Formation from…,” and received the Guinness World Record for the “longest title of a book” with 3,777 words and 26,021 characters. I described all species that exist on earth with a heart, right from the beginning of life on earth. I donated the book for free to 966 underprivileged and poor students in India and Kyrgyzstan for improving the reading habits of the younger generation.
