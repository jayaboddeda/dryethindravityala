The Man You Know

Dr. Yethindra Vityala is a globally acclaimed Indian physician, researcher, and author who has made remarkable strides in medical science, specifically in the fields of endocrinology, neurology, oncology, infectious diseases, and public health. Renowned for his groundbreaking contributions at such a young age, he has earned global recognition through numerous world records, prestigious awards, and accolades. His academic endeavors and innovations, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic, have significantly influenced public health practices and contributed to better treatment protocols worldwide.

The Story You Don’t

Born in Warangal, Telangana, Dr. Yethindra has completed over 20 advanced medical courses from globally recognized institutions, published more than 80 research papers in high-impact journals, and authored two books that delve into the intricacies of medical research. As a multifaceted individual, he is celebrated for his humility, dedication to patient care, and passion for making healthcare accessible to all.

The Inspiration

Dr. Yethindra Vityala envisions a world where healthcare is not only effective and advanced but also inclusive, affordable, and accessible to people from all walks of life. His approach seamlessly integrates clinical excellence with scientific research, focusing on improving public health systems, addressing emerging infectious diseases, and providing innovative solutions to complex medical challenges. His long-term goal is to continue bridging the gap between research and practical healthcare, ensuring that the most cutting-edge treatments reach the communities that need them the most.

Medical Research

Dr. Yethindra has contributed to groundbreaking research in various fields of medicine. His work focuses on finding new treatments and repurposing drugs for diseases such as cancer, stroke, tuberculosis, and COVID-19.

  • Influence of Clinical, Angiographic, and Developmental Characteristics and COVID-19 Severity and Vaccination Status on Young Patients with Acute Myocardial Infarction (Journal of Mid-Life Health, 2024)
  • Rare case of complete gonadal dysgenesis in a female patient with primary amenorrhea and a 46XY karyotype (Clinical Case Reports, 2024)
  • Evaluation of Cardiovascular Diseases and Diabetes Mellitus as Predisposing Factors for Mortality in Patients with Coronavirus Disease 2019: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis (American Heart Journal, 2021)

Publications & Case Studies

Dr. Yethindra has authored two books and numerous publications in peer-reviewed journals. His studies have been cited globally, and his case reports on rare diseases have contributed significantly to medical literature.

  • Published over 80 articles across various fields of medical and scientific research.
  • Contributed to high-impact journals from reputable publishers like Wolters Kluwer, Wiley, Springer, and Elsevier.
  • Focused on rare case reports, vaccine design, drug safety and efficacy, socially impactful studies, and under-researched topics.

Awards & World Records

Dr. Yethindra is a 12-time world record holder and recipient of prestigious awards for his contributions to medicine and humanitarian efforts.

  • World Records: Longest book title, youngest person to write a trilingual book, and more.
  • Awards: Karmaveer Chakra Award, International Distinguished Young Researcher, Mahatma Gandhi National Award.

Lectures & Conferences

Dr. Yethindra has been a keynote speaker at multiple international medical conferences. He has delivered lectures on various topics, ranging from cancer treatment to viral diseases.

  • In Vitro Inhibitory Effect of Fluoxetine Against SARS-CoV-2 – Lublin International Medical Congress, Poland.

Have Any Questions About Me?

I specialize in medical research, including drug repurposing, clinical study reports, pharmacovigilance protocols, and in vitro and in vivo studies. My key focus areas include diseases such as cancer, COVID-19, stroke, and tuberculosis.

To collaborate with me, feel free to reach out via email at dryethindravityala@gmail.com or through the contact form on this website. I am open to partnerships in research, medical writing, and educational initiatives.

I have achieved 12 world records, including being recognized as the World’s Youngest Scientist in Medicine, holding the Guinness World Record for the longest title of a book, and being the youngest person to write a trilingual book.

My vision is to extend my knowledge to students and peers across the world while focusing on finding and repurposing drugs for various diseases. I am also committed to global issues such as child sexual abuse prevention, climate crisis management, and promoting Sustainable Development Goals.
